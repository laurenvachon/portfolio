Sunset Psychic App Concept

UX & UI Design.

Sketch | InVision | Mobile App | Concept

Created December 2017

Sunset Psychic is a sunrise & sunset prediction app geared toward photographers of all skill level. Some of the features include:

  • Searchable locations - instead of looking at a huge map of the entire US, I wanted to be able to pinpoint specific locations
    • Current location
    • Search all of the US by town or city
    • Recently searched
    • Default location - when reopening app, it will go straight here
  • Time of sunrise/sunset
  • Weather at time of sunrise/sunset
  • Visual representation of prediction (hot/cold) and funny anecdote to go with it
  • Notifications - when it predicts a good sunrise/sunset in whatever range you choose
  • Save default location

The Challenge

As a landscape photography hobbyist, I’m constantly chasing after the perfect sunrise or sunset. In fact, I often find myself waking up at ungodly hours or skipping out on dinner to capture the perfect shot. However, there’s nothing worse than rolling out of bed at 5 am or driving over an hour only to find out the weather isn’t cooperating with my carefully planned photoshoot. This couldn’t be an uncommon problem, so I began my search on the web where I stumbled upon SunsetWX, a website that uses meteorology to make sunrise and sunset predictions.

While I love the accuracy of SunsetWx, it’s very confusing and cumbersome to use. It displays as a tiny map, isn’t searchable based on location and is challenging to get an accurate reading of a specific location.

Below is a screenshot of SunsetWX's interface.

The Solution

Sunset Psychic packages the data gathered from SunsetWx and puts it into a user friendly app that allows the user to plug in a location (anywhere or current), and instantly get the sunrise and sunset predictions. The view also shows the time of sunset/sunrise, temperature, weather condition, and a funny saying based on the probability.

The sunrise/sunset prediction is based on a 5 tier scale denoted by temperature - “hot” being the best, and “cold” being the worst.

  • Hot: 90% - 100%
  • Warmer: 80% - 89%
  • Warm: 70% - 79%
  • Cool: 60% - 69%
  • Cold: 0% - 59%

You’ll notice as the scale changes, so does the color palette on the app.

The user can easily switch screens from sunrise to sunset by swiping left and right. To change the location, click the location shown on the bottom right corner.

The app also has the ability to send you notifications based on how good the prediction is. It allows the user to choose if they want it to be notified on weekends, weekdays, or both. Also, you can select on a scale when you want to be notified. All located in the settings menu.

Research & Ideation

The Audience

After doing a lot of research on the target demographic, I identified two personas that represent the market.

James

  • Age: 35
  • Occupation: Project Manager
  • Gender: Male
  • Income: $80k+
  • Education: Masters Degree
  • Family Status: Married with 2 Children
  • Tech Comfort: Medium
Bio

James is a Project Manager at a software company, and is a father of two young kids - needless to say, he’s running around constantly and has little free time. When he does find a moment to himself, it’s typically before the kids wake up or after they go to bed. Since he’s running around exhausted, sleep is his best friend.

James loves landscape photography, but values his sleep, so it’s challenging for him to sacrifice his sleep in the hope that he will catch a good sunrise.

Goals & Tasks
  1. Get the weather, time, and prediction all in one area
  2. Get a notification only when there’s going to be a good sunrise/sunset

She plans her days around “golden hour” and the sunrises/sunsets that will make for the best photos. She’s constantly a few hours short on sleep, and itching for her next destination.

User Testing

User Testing

Goal:

To successfully have the user navigate within the app to find the sunrise and sunset predictions in Boston, change the location to Denver, get current location, Find where notification settings are.

Methodology:

The usability test consisted of three users and a set of tasks written out to be followed by the user.

Tasks:

  • Task 1: Find sunrise in Boston
  • Task 2: Find sunset in Boston
  • Task 3: Change location to Denver
  • Task 4: Find where notification settings are
  • Task 5: Find current location

Key Findings

  • Too much info on one screen - have sunrise and sunset on their own pages
  • Add recent list of places, to make them more accessible
  • Settings eye doesn’t look like settings
  • Add ability to have a default location for when you open the app after the first time
